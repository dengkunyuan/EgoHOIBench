Abstract

Understanding the interaction between humans and objects has gained much attention in recent years. Existing human-object interaction (HOI) detection methods primarily focus on the third-person vision, ignoring a more intuitive manner from the egocentric view of HOI, namely Ego-HOI. This paper introduces an Ego-HOIBench, a new benchmark dataset for Egocentric HOI Detection in a first-person view captured by head-mounted devices. Our Ego-HOIBench comprises more than 27K egocentric images with explicit and high-quality annotations for Ego-HOI detection across 123 fine-grained interaction categories which contain a rich diversity of scenarios, object types, and hand configurations. In the egocentric view, objects of interest often suffer from severe visual occlusion due to the alignment of the observer’s viewpoint with the hand motion trajectory, making it more challenging to understand intricate interactions. To this end, we exploit the inherent structural feature of the hand and propose a Hand Geometric and Interactivity Refinement (HGIR) scheme that leverages hand pose and geometric information as valuable cues for interpreting interactions. Specifically, we extract global hand geometric features from the estimated pose proposals while refining interaction-specific appearance features using cross-hand pose visual cues. By aggregating the structural insights from geometric features and the rich context and surface details offered by visual features, we obtain a more robust and precise interaction representation, significantly improving the performance of Ego-HOI detection. Our approach is simple, versatile, and effective, which can be easily applied to representative HOI baselines in a plug-and-play manner and achieve state-of-the-art results on the Ego-HOIBench.

Abstract

Understanding the interaction between humans and objects has gained much attention in recent years. Existing human-object interaction (HOI) detection methods mainly focus on the third-person perspectives, overlooking a more intuitive manner from the egocentric view of HOI, namely Ego-HOI. This paper introduces an Ego-HOIBench, a new benchmark dataset for Egocentric HOI Detection in a first-person view captured by head-mounted devices. Our Ego-HOIBench comprises more than 27K egocentric images with high-quality annotations for Ego-HOI detection across 123 fine-grained interaction categories which contain a rich diversity of scenarios, object types, and hand configurations in daily activities. From the egocentric view, objects of interest frequently suffer from severe visual occlusion due to the narrowness of the observer’s view, making it more challenging to understand intricate interactions. To this end, we exploit the inherent structural feature of the hand and propose a Hand Geometry and Interactivity Refinement (HGIR) scheme that leverages hand pose and geometric information as valuable cues for interpreting interactions. Specifically, the HGIR scheme explicitly extracts global hand geometric features from the estimated hand pose proposals and refines the interaction-specific features using pose-prompt attention. This enables the model to obtain a robust and precise interaction representation, significantly improving the Ego-HOI detection capability. Our approach is simple, versatile, and effective, which can be easily applied to representative HOI baselines in a plug-and-play manner and achieve state-of-the-art results on the Ego-HOIBench.
